GETTING STARTED: Building Your First Tournament


Building your own tournament can be extremely intimidating. If this is your first time trying, don't worry, cause we've got you covered. Battle Buddy is your friend for creating tournaments quickly and efficiently. All you need is:

A Battle Buddy Login
League of Legends Username
Tournament Name
Players and their Teams

Once you're logged in, go to the Create Tournament page, and fill in the information. When you hit submit, Battle Buddy will automatically generate a tournament event for you. Then, return to the tournaments page, where your new tournament will be listed! If you've used Battle Buddy before, then your old tournaments will show up here as well. From there, you can choose to edit your teams, players, and anything else you'd like about your tournament! As soon as you're ready to go, click the green "Play!" button next to your tournament name, and your bracket will be automatically launched for you to input your game standings and results.


FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

How do I log in?

To log in, click the words "Log In" in the top right corner of the page. Or click here.

Will others be able to see my tournaments?

Nope! Your tournaments will not be made public. Not everyone wants tournaments to be displayed to the world, so we've made it so you can keep them to yourself ;)

How many people do I need for a tournament?

For larger public tournaments, ideally you would want somewhere between 8-12 teams to have a long running tournament. However, no tournament is ever too small, so play to your heart's content!

Who edited this file?

Probably the most common question asked even though we made it very clear to always pull current commits.

Will my tournament results be saved?

Yep! You'll be able to see all tournaments you've hosted, as well as the results. You can access them from the Tournament Page.


Common Challenges: Roadblocks To Be Aware Of

Not everyone has experience runninng tournaments, but it can be extremely fun and even more rewarding. Of course, this reward does not come without some hard work. We asked a couple of individuals who have ample tournament-running experience, and asked them to compile a list of the most common or pressing challenges that they've faced. They also shared some advice in hopes to see others' tournament dreams succeed!

Challenges

Unsportsmanlike Players
These guys will get in your way. They're not at every tournament, but people can get real salty when it comes to video games. Do your best to deal with their core concern, and remind them that tournaments are supposed to be a fun and inclusive environment.

Balancing Complaints
This isn't a perfect world; not every tournament is going to be exceptionally balanced. Do your best to balance the brackets as best you can, but some things just can't be helped. As long as you explain to those who are unhappy and are transparent about your bracket building, most players will understand and carry on as normal.

Games Running Overtime
This is one of the more difficult issues to deal with. Games may take longer than anicitpated to finish, and sometimes this will hold up the bracket. Make sure that when you're creating your scheudle, leave some sort of buffer to time (we like to disguise these as "between game breaks") in preparation for game holdups. Also, be sure to alot enough time to each game slot accordingly. We suggest an hour and fifteen minutes per game, with a fifteen minute break in between.

Internet Connection
If your participants need to all be in a central local, have them arrive early to set up internet connections. We all know that good ping is cruicial to any tournamenet experience, so be prepared for heavy bandwith.

Advice

Don't Rush Yourself
Organizing isn't easy! Not everyone knows how to handle everything all at once, so pace yourself. Remind yourself of the important issues and the things that immediately need to be taken care of. Drink water and keep yourself refreshed so that players can always count on you for direction.

No Hard Feelings
There's no denying it- you're going to run into some bad potatoes. That's ok! Keep your head up and always be the better person. Do your best to see every conflict from a neutral standpoint, and of course, do not try to engage in anything. As an orgranizer, your goal is get your tournament finished as smoothly as possible, and at the end of the day you have worked the hardest to make it come to life.

Tournaments are Meant to be Fun
Have fun! Spectate the games, engage with players. Even though you're running it all, it's still an environment for you and your participants to enjoy. You've all earned it.
